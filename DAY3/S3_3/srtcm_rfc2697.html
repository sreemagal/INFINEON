RFC 2697 · Heinanen & Guerin · September 1999

srTCMSingle rate three color marker.

Policing · CIR · CBS · EBS

A traffic meter that classifies each arriving packet as green, yellow, or red. One rate, two burst budgets, two cascaded token buckets. The scheme lets a flow sustain its contracted rate, absorb reasonable bursts at full fidelity, and use spare capacity opportunistically — without ever exceeding its long-run commitment.

01The parameters

CIR
Committed Information Rate — bytes per second. The single token-generation rate that feeds both buckets.
CBS
Committed Burst Size — bytes. Capacity of the first bucket Tc. The burst allowance that is guaranteed to be marked green.
EBS
Excess Burst Size — bytes. Capacity of the second bucket Te. Absorbs overflow from Tc; covers packets marked yellow.
Tc, Te
The two bucket fill levels, in tokens. Both are initialised full: Tc(0) = CBS, Te(0) = EBS.

02The mechanism

Tokens arrive at rate CIR. They fill Tc first. When Tc is already at CBS, the new tokens spill into Te, up to EBS. When both are full, further tokens are discarded. This overflow-from-Tc-into-Te is the one structural idea that distinguishes srTCM from a plain token bucket.

On packet arrival of size B bytes, in color-blind mode, the meter runs a three-step test:

01
If Tc ≥ B → mark green, decrement Tc by B. The packet is within the committed budget.
02
Else if Te ≥ B → mark yellow, decrement Te by B. The committed budget is spent; the packet draws from the excess reserve.
03
Else → mark red. No token is consumed; both reserves are empty. The packet is dropped, or forwarded with high drop precedence, depending on the action attached to the marker.

03Try it

Watch the token stream feed Tc. When Tc saturates, notice the overflow threading into Te. Send packets and see which bucket covers them — the packet flashes green, yellow, or red at the decision point. Try a bursty arrival on top of a well-filled excess bucket: you'll get a run of yellows after the greens run out, before reds appear.

04Dimensioning example

A 10 Mbps customer link metered at CIR = 5 Mbps (625,000 B/s), CBS = 16 KB, EBS = 32 KB.

Idle
Both buckets full. A 32 KB burst arrives: 16 KB is covered by Tc (green), the next 16 KB draws from Te (yellow). The packet immediately after is red until tokens refill.
Contract
Steady 5 Mbps load keeps Tc continuously at the drain rate; Tc mostly hovers around zero and Te stays low. Every packet is green; no yellow or red is produced.
Overload
Source offers 8 Mbps. Tc empties and stays empty; Te follows. In steady state, ⅝ of packets mark green (CIR fraction), the rest are red once Te drains. Yellow only appears during the transient.

05Color-aware mode

The simulator above is color blind: every packet arrives unmarked and the meter chooses its color. In color-aware mode, packets arrive pre-coloured — typically by an upstream policer — and the meter can only downgrade them. A packet marked green upstream can be remarked yellow or red if its bucket is dry; a packet marked yellow cannot be upgraded to green even if Tc has capacity. Color-aware chaining is how differentiated services domains compose multiple policers without cross-contamination.

06Contrast with trTCM

srTCM shares one rate between the committed and excess buckets — the excess is a burst budget, not a second throughput tier. RFC 2698 two-rate TCM introduces a Peak Information Rate feeding Tp separately, so a flow can be allowed a sustained rate above CIR (marked yellow) up to PIR. Choose srTCM when the contract is "one rate, some burst tolerance"; choose trTCM when it's "one committed rate plus a higher peak rate".
