What CBS is doing to the FIFO queue

Credit-Based Shaping does not reorder frames inside the selected queue. The queue remains First-In, First-Out. CBS only decides when the head frame is allowed to leave.

  • When the head frame is selected, transmission starts if credit is non-negative.
  • While a CBS frame is transmitted, credit decreases using sendSlope.
  • When CBS has a frame waiting but is not allowed to transmit, credit increases using idleSlope.
  • After a frame creates negative credit, the next CBS frame waits until credit recovers to zero.

Formula card

credit >= 0 → CBS queue may be selected
credit < 0 → CBS queue must wait

sendSlope = idleSlope − portTransmitRate
hiCredit = maxInterferenceSize × idleSlope / portTransmitRate
loCredit = maxFrameSize × sendSlope / portTransmitRate

The animation uses byte credits. Frame sizes are treated as on-the-wire bytes for teaching clarity.

Scenario guide

Burst is shaped into paced departures

Several CBS frames arrive together. The first can leave immediately. Its transmission pushes credit negative. The following frames remain in FIFO order, but they are released only after credit climbs back to zero.

Interference builds positive credit

If a CBS frame is waiting while another frame is already occupying the egress port, CBS accumulates positive credit up to hiCredit. That positive credit permits a short CBS burst afterwards.

Positive credit reset

If the CBS queue becomes empty while credit is positive, the credit is reset to zero. CBS cannot hoard unused service forever.

Teaching interpretation

CBS is often easier to understand as a bandwidth account. A queue can send when its account is non-negative. Sending spends credit faster than it earns it, because the physical link is faster than the reserved rate. Waiting earns credit at the reserved rate.

This is a teaching animation, not a bit-exact implementation of a particular switch ASIC. It preserves the important CBS behaviors: non-negative selection, negative-credit recovery, positive-credit cap, FIFO preservation, and positive-credit reset on empty queue. Event arrivals are sorted by timestamp before simulation so the scenario timeline remains deterministic even if a future scenario is written out of order.
