Encoding rules: Every bit period has a mandatory mid-bit transition (clock). A 0 additionally has a transition at the start of the period. A 1 has no start-of-period transition. Count transitions per period: one = bit 1, two = bit 0.

Self-clocking

The mid-bit transition is present in every period regardless of data. The receiver locks its clock to this edge — no separate clock wire needed.

Polarity independent

Bit values are encoded as presence/absence of a transition, not as signal levels. Reversing the cable polarity does not corrupt decoded data.

DC balanced

Every period spends equal time at HIGH and LOW. No DC component accumulates regardless of the data pattern — important for transformer-coupled links.

Bounded bandwidth

Minimum one transition per period (all 1s), maximum two (all 0s). Worst-case signal frequency is exactly 2× the bit rate.
